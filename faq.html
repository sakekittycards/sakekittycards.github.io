Grading & Card Prep

We work with PSA, CGC, and Beckett. We pick the best fit for each card based on value, condition, and turnaround time. More on the Grading Prep page.
Most screening orders are completed within 2–3 business days after we receive your cards.
You'll get a pass/fail result by email. Cards that don't pass can be returned right away or held and shipped back with your graded cards — your choice.

We keep the process simple and controlled:

  1. You submit your cards for screening.
  2. We evaluate each card against grading standards.
  3. Cards that pass are approved for submission.
  4. We confirm your grading costs before moving forward.
  5. Payment is collected before approved cards are submitted.

Grading fees apply only to cards you approve for submission — no grading charges on cards that don't pass. If you added Card Prep, that fee is refunded for any card that doesn't pass screening.

Cards stay with us until grading fees are paid in full — and you'll see your grades when the cards return to you, not before.

Screening takes 2–3 business days after we receive your cards. Grading at PSA, CGC, or Beckett typically runs 3–8 weeks for standard tiers (express tiers are faster, premium tiers can take longer). We share an estimated timeline before submission and you can follow progress on our order tracker.
You cover inbound shipping (your cards to us) — we strongly recommend insured shipping with tracking and signature required for valuable cards. Once we receive them, we cover all shipping to and from the grading house, including required insurance. Return shipping back to you is included with your service, insured based on declared value with signature required on delivery. Risk of loss transfers to the carrier upon shipment, and we’ll help you file an insurance claim if anything goes wrong in transit.
Every card is photographed front and back at intake and again before return. Cards graded by PSA, CGC, or Beckett come back in tamper-evident slabs with a unique cert number — you can verify the cert directly on the grading house's website, which independently confirms the card's identity.
If a grading house determines a card is counterfeit, altered, trimmed, or otherwise can't be authenticated, the card is returned without a grade. The grading house typically refunds its grading fee in this case — we pass any refund through to you. Screening and Card Prep fees are not refunded, since that work was already performed in good faith.

You provide the declared value at submission, and you're responsible for its accuracy. If a grading company determines a card's value is higher and upgrades the service tier, the escalation fee is passed through to you at cost — it becomes part of the grading fees due before return.
